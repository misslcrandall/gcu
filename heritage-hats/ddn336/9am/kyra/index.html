Our History

Heritage Hats was born out of a love for tradition and craftsmanship. When we moved to Phoenix in 1981 from Rockford, Illinois, we became fascinated with the cowboy lifestyle and the culture of the Southwest. Under the guidance of master hatters, Steve Speros & George Falkedes, our creator Rich Glisson learned the art of cleaning and blocking western hats. We still use their original 1912 equipment, carrying forward their legacy with pride.

We started small, selling hats from a pickup truck at rodeos before opening our first retail store in 1983. From those humble beginnings, Heritage Hats grew into Arizona's largest hat store, with 3,200 square feet of retail space, two showrooms, and an on-site workshop where we continue to restore and create hats by hand. Over the years, our dedication to quality has led us to craft hats for everyone from local ranchers to well-known figures like Ronald Reagan & George Burns.

Our story is rooted in heritage, built on mentorship, tradition, & a passion for keeping the craft of hat-making alive.

FAQs

  • Regular cleaning: Use a soft brush (or damp terry cloth) to dust your hat in a circular, counterclockwise motion.
  • Handle with care: Always pick up or adjust your hat by the brim, not the crown, to avoid deforming it.
  • Storage matters: Never rest your hat on its brim—that will flatten it. Avoid leaving it in very hot places like a parked car.
  • Felt hats: For tougher stains, use a gum eraser or very fine (100-grit) sandpaper gently (moving counterclockwise).
  • Straw hats: Wipe with a soft cloth dipped in warm soapy water, then rinse with a barely damp cloth and air dry. Avoid soaking the straw.

Choosing the right hat depends on your lifestyle, climate, and personal style. If you spend time outdoors or need sun protection, wide-brim options like western or Panama hats are ideal, while felt styles work better in cooler weather. For a rugged, travel-ready look, try an Australian hat; for something timeless and refined, go with a fedora. Consider how the hat complements your wardrobe—western hats pair naturally with denim and boots, while fedoras and Panamas suit more polished or casual city looks. Brim width, material, and comfort all matter, so try on different styles and let the fit, feel, and look guide your choice.
