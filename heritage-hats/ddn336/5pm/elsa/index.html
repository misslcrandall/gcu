Hat Care Tips

At Heritage Hats, we know a hat is an investment and a keepsake. Proper care protects its style, memories, and sun protection. Follow these steps to keep yours looking its best for years.

General Care:

  1. Dust regularly: Use a soft-bristled brush to remove dust, or gently rub with a damp terrycloth towel in a circular, counterclockwise motion.
  2. Handle properly: Always take your hat on and off by holding the front and back of the brim next to the crown bend never by the crown itself.
  3. Store correctly: Set your hat upside down on its crown or on a stand, not on the brim, to prevent flattening.
  4. Avoid heat: Keep hats out of very hot areas, such as inside an enclosed vehicle, to prevent damage.

Felt Hats:

  1. Remove stains: Use a gum eraser on deep stains after brushing.
  2. For stubborn marks: Lightly sand with 100-grit paper in a counterclockwise direction. Do not press into the hat or press the hat into the paper.

Straw Hats:

  1. Clean gently: Wipe with a soft cloth dipped in warm suds made from synthetic detergent or mild soap.
  2. Rinse: Use another cloth barely dampened with plain water to remove soap residue.
  3. Dry properly: Allow to air dry. Do not get a straw hat excessively wet, as this may damage it.

HAT GUIDE

Outback Hats

Australian/Outback hats stand out for their wide brims, weather-resistant materials, and durability, offering superior sun protection for rugged outdoor conditions.

Fedora Felt Hats

The fedora, introduced in 1882 as a women's hat, became a male fashion icon in the 20th century and remains stylish for both men and women today.

Fedora Straw Hats

The fedora, introduced in 1882 as a women's hat, became a 20th-century style icon for men and women. Originally felt, it's now also made in straw.

Flat Caps

Flat caps, created in 1938 and worn by soldiers, celebrities, and the hip-hop scene, remain iconic, stylish classics today.

Panama Hats

Panama hats are finely handwoven straw hats from Ecuador, known for their lightweight feel, sun protection, and timeless style.

Western Felt Hats

The western cowboy hat, created by John B. Stetson in 1865, is an iconic symbol of the Wild West, still worn by cowboys and enthusiasts today.

Western Straw Hats

The western cowboy hat is an American icon. Heritage Hats offers western straw hats with vented crowns for sun protection and UV-blocking materials.
